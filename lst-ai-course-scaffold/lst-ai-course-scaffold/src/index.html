Local Systems Theory in AI

A year-long course on building AI systems that survive contact with human-scale reality.

This is not prompt trick school. This is systems training for human-AI loops: prompts, models, memory, tools, feedback, governance, constraints, failure, and repair.

Operating law

Bring the AI loop closer → reveal the friction → govern the decision → repair the system.

AI becomes locally intelligent when its outputs remain close enough to verify, constrained enough to stay honest, and governed enough to repair when wrong.

Course thesis

AI is a node inside a local system.

The course asks what happens when AI enters real loops of attention, work, judgment, evidence, trust, consequence, and repair.

LST-AI(A) = {H, M, Cx, L, F, G, C, T, E, R}

Human node, model node, context, local loop, friction, governance, constraints, time, externality, and repair.

Year structure

Same spine. Different technical depth.

Students move from local AI mapping to compression, friction diagnosis, proximity restoration, governance, material constraint testing, temporal design, anti-scale, and final decompression.
